Understanding the Problem

Traditional spacraft navigation uses Earth-based resources and has proved to be very reliable in many successful space missions. However, this technology can sometimes fail to meet the requirements of real time navigation in the following situations.

Super Expensive

and a complicated system for accurate transmission and reception of radio waves.

Possible Communication Delay

due to unavoidable traffic or technical glitches.

Huge Human Effort

to constantly observe, track, and record the location.

Solution

Cubesat Interplanetary Light Navigation (CION) aims to provide automation to solve the space navigation problems above. Without and human assistance or complex 3-D triangulations, this method relies on the images captured by the Cubesat cameras to self-navigate to the destination in following ways.

Mapping 3D Images

on 2-dimension using photographs taken by Cubestats in space (phone camera and 3D space-like arrangement in our case).

Python Image Recognition Algorithm

to obtain the astronomical body on two-dimensionals and calculate their distances on picture.

Deriving and Coding the Mathematical Algorithm

to calculate the unknown position of the Cubesat using the True Multi Lateration principle for three obtained bodies and the ratio between actual distance and 2-dimensional distance (can be derived using the focal length of the Cubesat camera).
